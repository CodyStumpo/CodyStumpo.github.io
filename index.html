I fiddle around with applied math problems.

Repos related to Baseball

MLB Playoff Series (R) - Markov Chain to compute a team's chance of winning a major league baseball playoff series given starting pitchers, bullpen, and lineup strength vs. LHP or RHP

Simple WAR calculator (R) - a Shiny app you can use to evaluate the Wins Above Replacement of a hitter or pitcher given his performance in key aspects of the game. Helps you get a sense for how the elements interact to create value.

Other Repos

2008 Presidential Election (Mathematica) - Code for a series of blog posts on Intrade, a prediction market. Combining portfolio simulation with the prediction market, I got a responsive, dynamic, and accurate predictor of the race. Also got it to publish live updates. Since Intrade is out of business (because regulators) the blog posts themselves are not well-archived but are more or less to be found here. It'll make more sense if you scroll to the bottom and read up in chronological order.

Chipotle Nutrition (R) - Annoyed by the NY Times calorie-shaming analysis of data around 5000 detailed orders, I delved into the full nutritional content. Also published as R Markdown writeup with some nifty visualizations

DataKind (Python + R) - volunteered to produce recommender system for TechSoup, a non-profit that coordinates IT donations to other non-profits. Our team used a mix of clustering and random forests to generate recommendations for users that matched purchase behavior much better than any current approach.
